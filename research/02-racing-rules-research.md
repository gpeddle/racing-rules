# Phase 2: Racing Rules of Sailing -- Research

**Date:** 2026-05-11

## Summary

The Racing Rules of Sailing (RRS) 2025-2028 is a structured, hierarchical rulebook published by World Sailing every four years. It contains 7 Parts, ~90 numbered rules, ~25 definitions, and multiple appendices. For a US-centric learning app, the most important content spans Parts 1-4 (the rules competitors encounter on the water), key definitions, and US Sailing prescriptions. Rich supplementary content exists in official case books and appeals that can serve as scenario-based learning material.

### What Matters Most for Learners

Not all parts are equally important for a learning app. Priority tiers:

**Tier 1 -- Must learn (on-the-water rules):**
- Definitions (especially: clear ahead/astern, overlap, keep clear, leeward/windward, proper course, room, zone, mark-room, tack/starboard/port)
- Part 2: When Boats Meet (Rules 10-23) -- this is the core of racing rules knowledge
- Key rules from Part 1 (Rule 2: Fair Sailing, Rule 5: Anti-doping, Rule 6: betting)

**Tier 2 -- Should learn (race conduct):**
- Part 3: Conduct of a Race (starting, recalls, finishing)
- Part 4 selected rules (Rule 42: propulsion, Rule 44: penalties/turns)

**Tier 3 -- Good to know (procedural):**
- Part 5: Protests and hearings (how to file, what happens)
- Race signals

**Tier 4 -- Reference only (for the app, not for quizzing):**
- Parts 6-7 (entry, organization)
- Most appendices

## Key Definitions

These defined terms have precise meanings in the rules. Misunderstanding them is a primary source of on-the-water confusion.

| Definition | Why It Matters |
|---|---|
| **Clear Ahead / Clear Astern / Overlap** | Determines which boat-on-boat rules apply |
| **Keep Clear** | Core obligation -- what it means to stay out of another boat's way |
| **Leeward / Windward** | Determines right-of-way on same tack |
| **Mark-Room** | New in context of 2025-2028 changes; defines what room is owed at marks. Now stops when a boat "leaves the mark astern" |
| **Proper Course** | Now defined as completing the leg "as soon as possible" (changed from "finish") |
| **Room** | Space needed to maneuver promptly in a seamanlike way |
| **Zone** | Area within 3 hull lengths of a mark; triggers mark-room rules |
| **Tack (starboard or port)** | Fundamental to Rule 10 (opposite tacks) |
| **Obstruction** | Triggers Rules 19-20 |
| **Continuing Obstruction** | New definition in 2025-2028: an obstruction a boat passes alongside for at least 3 hull lengths |
| **Committee** | New defined term in 2025-2028: means race committee, protest committee, or technical committee |

## Part 2: When Boats Meet -- The Core Content

This is where most learning effort should focus. These rules govern right-of-way and are the source of most protests.

### Section A: Right of Way

| Rule | Title | Essence |
|---|---|---|
| 10 | On Opposite Tacks | Port-tack boat keeps clear of starboard-tack boat |
| 11 | On the Same Tack, Overlapped | Windward boat keeps clear of leeward boat |
| 12 | On the Same Tack, Not Overlapped | Boat clear astern keeps clear of boat clear ahead |
| 13 | While Tacking | Boat tacking keeps clear until on a close-hauled course |

### Section B: General Limitations

| Rule | Title | Essence |
|---|---|---|
| 14 | Avoiding Contact | Even right-of-way boats must avoid contact when possible |
| 15 | Acquiring Right of Way | When you gain right of way, give other boat room to keep clear |
| 16 | Changing Course | Right-of-way boat changing course must give other boat room |
| 17 | On the Same Tack, Proper Course | Limits on how far leeward boat can sail above proper course when overlapped |

### Section C: At Marks and Obstructions

| Rule | Title | Essence |
|---|---|---|
| 18 | Mark-Room | The most complex and most protested rule. Governs who owes room at marks within the zone |
| 19 | Room to Pass an Obstruction | Right-of-way at obstructions |
| 20 | Room to Tack at an Obstruction | Hailing for room to tack |

### Section D: Other Rules

| Rule | Title | Essence |
|---|---|---|
| 21 | Exoneration | When a boat is exempt from penalty despite breaking a rule |
| 22 | Capsized, Anchored, or Aground | Keep clear of boats in distress |
| 23 | Interfering with Another Boat | Don't interfere if you're not racing or are on a different leg |

## Rule 18 (Mark-Room) -- Deep Dive

Rule 18 is the most complex, most protested, and most frequently misunderstood rule. The 2025-2028 edition significantly rewrote Rules 18.2 and 18.3.

### Key Concepts

1. **The Zone:** 3 hull lengths from a mark. Entering the zone is the trigger event for mark-room obligations.

2. **Overlap at zone entry:** The "snapshot" moment. When the first boat reaches the zone, the overlap status at that instant determines mark-room obligations.

3. **Rule 18.2 (Giving Mark-Room):**
   - 18.2(a): Same tack, overlapped -- outside boat gives inside boat mark-room (restructured in 2025-2028 so this common case comes first)
   - 18.2(a)(2): Not overlapped -- boat that has not reached zone gives mark-room to boat that reached zone first
   - Obligations continue even if overlap changes after zone entry

4. **Rule 18.3 (Tacking in the Zone):**
   - Major 2025-2028 change: tacking to starboard in the zone when another boat is already fetching the mark on starboard no longer earns you mark-room under 18.2(c)
   - Prevents the tactical abuse of tacking into mark-room

5. **Common Misunderstandings:**
   - Thinking you can break an overlap after entering the zone to escape obligations
   - Not understanding when Rule 18 does NOT apply (opposite tacks on a beat)
   - Confusing mark-room with room (mark-room includes room to sail to the mark, room to round, and room to sail proper course)

### Why This Matters for the App

Rule 18 scenarios are ideal quiz content. They involve:
- Spatial reasoning (boat positions, overlaps, zones)
- Temporal reasoning (what was true at zone entry vs. now)
- Conditional logic (which sub-rule applies given the situation)

Visual, scenario-based questions with boat diagrams are the natural format.

## Penalty Turns (Rule 44)

Another commonly misunderstood area:

- When a boat breaks a rule, she can take a penalty (one or two turns)
- Must sail "well clear of other boats as soon after the incident as possible"
- Turns must be made "promptly"
- Late turns don't count -- boats can still be disqualified even though they spun
- A one-turn penalty (360 degrees) applies when in the zone and on the finishing leg
- A two-turn penalty (720 degrees) applies in other situations

## 2025-2028 Changes from 2021-2024

Key changes relevant to a learning app:

| Change | Impact |
|---|---|
| Rule 18.2 restructured | Same-tack overlap case now comes first; clearer reading order |
| Rule 18.3 rewritten | Prevents tacking-to-starboard abuse at marks |
| Mark-Room definition updated | Stops when boat "leaves the mark astern" (was less clear) |
| Proper Course redefined | Now "to complete the leg as soon as possible" (was "to finish") |
| Continuing Obstruction defined | New definition: obstruction passed alongside for 3+ hull lengths |
| Committee defined | New term covering race/protest/technical committees |
| Part 5 restructured and renumbered | Simplified protest requirements; all Part 5 rule references changed |
| Written protest simplified | Complicated conditional info requirements removed |
| Numbered sub-items added throughout | Long sentences broken into numbered items for clarity |

## US Sailing Prescriptions

US Sailing adds prescriptions that apply only to racing in the United States. These rank as rules for US events.

### What Are Prescriptions?

- Rules adopted by a national authority (US Sailing) that supplement or modify World Sailing rules
- Apply only within that country's jurisdiction
- Published alongside the World Sailing rules in the US edition

### Key US Prescriptions (2025-2028)

- Race committee must ensure notice of race and sailing instructions are readily available to each boat
- No fees shall be charged for protests or requests for redress
- Additional prescriptions modify specific rules (the full list is in the [US Sailing Prescriptions PDF](https://www.ussailing.org/wp-content/uploads/2024/11/US-Sailing-Prescriptions-for-2025-2028-10.29.2024.pdf))

### Implications for the App

- The app should teach World Sailing rules as the base
- US Prescriptions should be clearly marked as US-specific additions
- This architecture leaves room for future internationalization

## Official Case Books and Appeals

These are rich sources of scenario-based content for a learning app.

### World Sailing Case Book

- ~140 scenarios with authoritative interpretations
- Published by World Sailing for 40+ years
- Updated annually (new cases added each November)
- Status: "authoritative interpretations and explanations" (not rules themselves)
- Many cases based on actual appeals
- Available as PDF from [World Sailing](https://www.sailing.org/documents/caseandcall/case-book.php)

### US Sailing Appeals Book

- US-specific appeal decisions
- Published for each 4-year rules cycle: [Appeals Book 2025-2028](https://www.ussailing.org/wp-content/uploads/2025/01/Appeals-Book-for-2025-2028_1.1.2025.pdf)
- Searchable on [RacingRulesOfSailing.org](https://www.racingrulesofsailing.org/) by selecting US Prescriptions
- Includes detailed fact patterns, decisions, and rule citations

### RYA Case Book

- UK-based cases, also available on RRS.org
- Additional scenarios from a different national perspective

### Content Opportunity

Each case/appeal is essentially a ready-made quiz question:
- **Fact pattern** = scenario description (with boat diagram)
- **Question** = which boat broke a rule? / what should the decision be?
- **Answer** = the official ruling with rule citations and explanation

This is a massive library of expert-validated educational content that could be adapted for interactive use.
