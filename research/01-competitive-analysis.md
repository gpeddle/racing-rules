# Competitive Analysis: Racing Rules of Sailing Apps and Resources

**Date:** 2026-05-11

## Summary

The market for Racing Rules of Sailing (RRS) learning tools is small and fragmented. The dominant player is US Sailing's own free app, which serves as a digital rulebook with some educational content. No app currently focuses on structured pedagogy -- quizzing, spaced repetition, progressive mastery -- for learning the racing rules. This represents a clear gap.

---

## Mobile Apps

### 1. US Sailing Racing Rules (Official)

- **Platforms:** iOS, Android
- **Price:** Free (requires US Sailing membership)
- **Developer:** US Sailing
- **Links:** [App Store](https://apps.apple.com/us/app/us-sailing-racing-rules/id1536827107) | [Google Play](https://play.google.com/store/apps/details?id=org.ussailing.ussailingracingrules)

**What it does:**
- Full searchable text of RRS 2025-2028, available offline
- Protest submission form (no paper needed)
- Whiteboard for drawing protest diagrams, exportable as images or motion graphics
- In-app access to Dave Perry's "Understanding the Racing Rules of Sailing" (in-app purchase)
- Dave Perry video explanations of rule changes
- Resource library with documents and links

**What it does NOT do:**
- No quizzing or practice scenarios
- No structured learning path or curriculum
- No progress tracking or mastery indicators
- No gamification

**User sentiment:** Generally positive reviews. Users appreciate the searchable rulebook and Dave Perry videos. This is a reference tool, not a learning tool.

**Takeaway:** This is the "official" digital rulebook. It is not a competitor for a learning/practice app -- it is complementary. A learning app could even reference it.

---

### 2. The Racing Rules of Sailing (Ezio Fonda)

- **Platforms:** iOS, Android (as "Le Regole di Regata - 109 Quiz")
- **Price:** Free
- **Developer:** Ezio Fonda (Italian Sailing Federation Umpire/National Judge)
- **Links:** [App Store](https://apps.apple.com/us/app/the-racing-rules-of-sailing/id431680643) | [Google Play](https://play.google.com/store/apps/details?id=com.eziofonda.rdr)

**What it does:**
- 109 quiz questions with diagrams
- Score tracking with points
- Scenario-based: user sees a pictured boat situation and decides which boat violated a rule
- Answers include rule explanations

**Problems:**
- Poorly translated from Italian -- spelling errors, incomplete sentences, some chapter titles still in Italian
- Some rule interpretations reported as incorrect by users
- No privacy data collection (positive)
- Appears unmaintained / not updated for 2025-2028 rules

**Takeaway:** Closest thing to a "learning app" that exists, but poor execution. The quiz-with-diagrams format is validated by its existence, but the quality bar is low. A well-executed English-language version with correct, up-to-date rules would clearly surpass it.

### 3. ASA's Sailing Challenge (American Sailing Association)

- **Platforms:** iOS, Android, Windows, Mac
- **Price:** Paid app
- **Developer:** American Sailing Association
- **Links:** [App Store](https://apps.apple.com/us/app/asas-sailing-challenge/id1109489005) | [Google Play](https://play.google.com/store/apps/details?id=com.ASA.SailingGamePaid)

**What it does:**
- Six interactive modules: Points of Sail, Apparent Wind, Sail Trim, Tacking/Jibing, Rules of the Road, Docking
- Gamified: sail around a course against the clock, earn 36 awards
- Used as a teaching aid by ASA instructors
- Covers COLREGS (rules of the road), NOT racing rules of sailing

**Limitations:**
- Targets beginner sailors, not racers
- Covers general right-of-way (COLREGS), not RRS
- No quiz/test format for rules specifically

**Takeaway:** Not a direct competitor -- different audience (beginners) and different rules (COLREGS vs. RRS). However, its gamification approach and use as a teaching aid offer design inspiration.

## Web-Based Resources

### 6. RacingRulesOfSailing.org

- **Link:** [racingrulesofsailing.org](https://www.racingrulesofsailing.org/)

**What it does:**
- Comprehensive index of racing rules with references to cases, calls, and national appeals
- Practice questions and trial quizzes (limited)
- Jury management system for events
- Event registration tool

**Takeaway:** Authoritative reference site, some quiz content. Not a structured learning tool. Desktop-oriented.

---

### 7. Speed & Smarts -- Learn The Racing Rules (David Dellenbaugh)

- **Links:** [speedandsmarts.com](https://www.speedandsmarts.com/learn-the-racing-rules/rule-quizzes) | [Buy Course](https://speedandsmarts.com/learn-the-racing-rules/buy-racing-rules-course)

**What it does:**
- Step-by-step online course with live sailing demos, 3D animations, knowledge checks
- Six modules with dozens of sections
- Progress tracking
- Quizzes from the newsletter available online
- Related webinar series ($55-60 for 4 sessions)

**Limitations:**
- Web-only, not mobile-optimized as an app
- Still references 2021-2024 rules (may be updated)
- Paid product (pricing not publicly listed)

**Takeaway:** This is the strongest existing educational offering for racing rules. It has structured curriculum, animations, and assessment. However, it is web-based (not mobile-native), likely expensive, and not gamified. A mobile app with similar pedagogical depth but modern UX (spaced repetition, bite-sized sessions, progress tracking) would differentiate.

---

### 8. Dave Perry's 100 Best Racing Rules Quizzes (US Sailing)

- **Link:** [US Sailing - Dave Perry Quizzes](https://www.ussailing.org/competition/rules-officiating/dave-perrys-100-best-racing-rules-quizzes/)

**What it does:**
- 100 scenario-based quiz questions with detailed explanations
- Available as PDFs through US Sailing
- Written by the leading rules authority

**Takeaway:** High-quality content, but delivered as static PDFs. Strong candidate for the type of content a mobile app should deliver interactively.

## Market Gap Analysis

```
                    Reference Tool    Learning Tool
                    (look up rules)   (practice & master rules)
                    
Mobile App          US Sailing app    Fonda app (poor quality)
                    (strong)          ** GAP **
                    
Web                 RRS.org           Speed & Smarts (paid, web-only)
                    (strong)          Dave Perry quizzes (static PDFs)
```

**The gap is clear:** There is no high-quality mobile app that helps sailors *learn and practice* the Racing Rules of Sailing through structured pedagogy, scenario-based quizzing, and progress tracking.

### What exists is either:
- **Reference tools** (US Sailing app, RRS.org) -- look up rules, not learn them
- **Poor quality** (Fonda app) -- right idea, bad execution
- **Wrong rules** (ASA, Marinus, Sailing Quiz HD) -- COLREGS, not RRS
- **Web-only and paid** (Speed & Smarts) -- good content, wrong format for mobile learners
- **Static content** (Dave Perry PDFs, scattered quizzes) -- no interactivity, no progress tracking

### Opportunity characteristics:
- No well-executed mobile learning app exists for RRS
- The racing sailing community is underserved -- serious racers study rules from books and PDFs
- Modern learning techniques (spaced repetition, scenario-based practice, gamification) have not been applied
- The US Sailing app is complementary, not competitive -- a learning app sends users to it for reference
- Content authority matters -- association with recognized rules experts (Perry, Dellenbaugh) would add credibility
- The 2025-2028 rules cycle just started, creating a natural window for a new product
